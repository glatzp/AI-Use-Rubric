# AI Authorship Alignment Evaluator

A lightweight tool for evaluating how AI was used during a task and whether that use aligns with the demands of the work.

---

## Overview

As AI tools become more common, a new problem is emerging across education, professional work, and creative fields:

**Silent delegation.**

Work can be completed fluently and quickly, but the underlying thinking, decision-making, and verification may be partially or fully outsourced to AI without the user realizing it.

This tool does not attempt to stop AI use.

Instead, it helps users answer a more important question:

> **Did I use AI in a way that fits what this task actually required?**

---

## What This Tool Does

The evaluator measures authorship behavior across four domains:

* **Agency** — Who defined the direction, constraints, and decisions?
* **Cognitive Engagement** — Did the user actively think through and interrogate ideas?
* **Evidentiary Control** — Who selected and verified the evidence?
* **Authorship Integrity** — Is the claim to authorship justified by the process?

Before evaluation, the user defines task conditions:

* Speed
* Quality
* Ownership
* Risk

The system then:

1. Calculates an expected authorship range based on those conditions
2. Measures actual authorship behavior using structured checklists
3. Compares the two to determine alignment

---

## Important Design Principles

* **This is not a grading tool**
* **Higher authorship is not always better**
* **AI use is not penalized**
* **Alignment to task demands is the goal**

A low-authorship result may be completely appropriate for a low-risk, high-speed task.

A high-authorship result may be required for work involving accountability, accuracy, or public responsibility.

---

## Why This Matters

AI is not just changing how work is produced—it is changing:

* how decisions are made
* how responsibility is distributed
* how authorship is understood

In education, this affects:

* student learning
* academic integrity
* assessment validity

In professional contexts, this affects:

* decision quality
* accountability
* risk exposure

This tool makes those tradeoffs visible.

---

## Use Cases

This tool is intentionally simple in interaction but flexible in application.

It can be used:

* In middle school through higher education
* For classroom assignments or AI literacy instruction
* By professionals evaluating AI-assisted work
* During writing, research, planning, or design workflows
* As a reflective tool after completing a task

---

## How It Works (High-Level)

* Each domain uses a structured checklist
* Users select indicators and disqualifiers
* The system calculates the highest defensible level
* A confidence check can adjust the result
* Final output compares:

  * **Observed authorship behavior**
  * **Expected range based on task conditions**

---

## What This Tool Is Not

* It does not detect AI use
* It does not judge intent
* It does not replace human judgment

It is a **reflection and alignment tool**, not an enforcement system.

---

## Future Direction

Potential extensions include:

* saving evaluations over time
* pattern tracking across tasks
* classroom or team-level analytics
* integration into instructional or workflow systems

---

## Final Note

This tool is built on a simple premise:

> Using AI well is not about avoiding it or maximizing it.
> It is about knowing when and how to stay in control.
